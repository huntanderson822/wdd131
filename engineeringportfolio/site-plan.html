Overview

Purpose

The purpose of this site is to showcase the professional work of mechanical engineer Tricity Anderson. The portfolio will highlight her skills, engineering projects, and accomplishments so potential employers and collaborators can quickly understand what she can do. This website will act as a polished, central place she can share on résumés, LinkedIn, and applications.

Audience

The primary audience includes engineering recruiters, hiring managers, and technical interviewers who need a clear overview of Tricity’s capabilities. A secondary audience includes professors, classmates, and industry professionals who want to see examples of her projects and problem-solving process. These visitors are busy and technical, so the content will be concise, professional, and easy to navigate.

Dynamic elements

I will use JavaScript to dynamically generate and control the project content on the site. All project data will be stored in an array of objects inside an ES module (projects.js), which will be imported into the home page and the projects page. On the home page, JavaScript will filter and display a few featured projects and update a “highlight” text area about Tricity when the user clicks a button. On the projects page, JavaScript will render all project cards into the DOM and let users filter them by category using array methods like filter and forEach. The scripts will use multiple functions, DOM selection and modification, event listeners, and conditional branching (for example, showing a “no projects found” message or styling recent projects differently). I will also include a simple light/dark theme toggle controlled with JavaScript to demonstrate additional DOM interaction.

Paragraph Font: Roboto, sans-serif

Roboto is highly readable on screens and pairs well with Poppins. It will be used for body text and longer project descriptions.

Content

Home page

The home page will introduce Tricity as a mechanical engineer and give a quick overview of who she is and what she does. It will include:

  • A hero section with a short introduction, title (Mechanical Engineer), and a professional photo or engineering-related image.
  • A brief summary of her focus areas (for example: product design, robotics, thermal systems) and her approach to problem solving.
  • A “Featured Projects” section where a few key projects are displayed dynamically with JavaScript.
  • A skills snapshot listing tools and technologies such as SolidWorks, MATLAB, FEA, and prototyping experience.
  • A small contact area with links to email and LinkedIn so visitors can reach out.

Projects page

The projects page will be the main portfolio area where visitors can browse all of Tricity’s projects and filter them by category. It will include:

  • An introduction describing what kind of projects are shown (class projects, internships, personal builds, etc.).
  • Filter controls (for example: “All”, “Robotics”, “CAD/Design”, “Thermal/Fluids”) that are connected to JavaScript.
  • A grid of project cards generated from the JavaScript data. Each card will show the project title, year, category, a short description, and relevant skills used.
  • A conditional message that appears if no projects match the selected filter.
